Flagship Project

Patent-pending force-feedback control architecture for simulated rotary-wing aircraft

Designed a software-driven approach for translating helicopter trim, force-trim release, and automation logic into commercially available force-feedback controls.

  • Problem: no broadly usable method for reproducing realistic helicopter trim and autopilot behavior across consumer force-feedback hardware.
  • Built: a C# application using simulator telemetry and control-state logic to interpret trim inputs, automation states, and control feedback behavior.
  • Why it matters: demonstrates systems reasoning, aviation-domain depth, and the ability to turn a fragmented technical problem into a coherent product architecture.
